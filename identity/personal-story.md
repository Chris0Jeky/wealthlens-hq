## Why This Matters

- The widening participation work is the "emotional proof" that makes everything credible and non-performative.
- Personal story should be scaffolding for structural argument, never the building itself (10-20% personal, 80-90% structural).
- The most effective narrative structure is progression, not redemption: Constraint -> Craft -> Contradiction -> Turn -> Commitment.

## Themes to Cover (from research)

Write about any or all of these — whatever is true and feels right:

### Housing and Wealth
- What was housing like growing up? Renting? Owning? Moving?
- When did you first understand that wealth and housing were connected?
- What does the London housing market feel like from the inside?

### Wages and Opportunity
- What did "career" look like in your family?
- When did you realise that effort alone doesn't explain outcomes?
- What was the gap between what you were told about meritocracy and what you experienced?

### Education and Social Mobility
- What was your route into university? Were you the first in your family?
- What did you see as a Student Ambassador and Student Voice Leader?
- What do you see now in Widening Participation work — what do the kids you talk to face?

### The Contradiction
- You build trading systems AND you work with underprivileged kids. You understand financial markets from the inside AND you see their effects on the outside. What's that like?
- When did the contradiction become too big to ignore?

### The Turn
- What specifically triggered the shift? Was it Gary's videos? A conversation? A data point?
- What did you feel when you realised your engineering skills could be used differently?

### The Commitment
- Why open source? Why free? Why non-partisan?
- What do you want WealthLens to do that nothing else does?

## Narrative Arc (Template)

> I grew up [X]. I didn't [Y]. I studied computer science at Middlesex and got a First. I published research in Springer. I spent 15 months at GE Digital building security pipelines for AWS. I co-built a trading platform. I have 4,000 commits on a developer tool I built alone.
>
> Now I work in Widening Participation at Middlesex, going into schools and talking to kids who remind me of [Z]. I show them what's possible. But I also see the data on what's likely — and the gap between those two things is [A].
>
> [What changed]. [What I'm building now]. [Why it matters].

## Content Hooks (Once Written)

The personal story unlocks these content angles:

1. **LinkedIn launch post:** "Why I'm building WealthLens UK" — personal story + mission + call to action.
2. **Twitter/X manifesto thread:** The story in 5-7 tweets.
3. **Outreach emails:** "I work in widening participation because..." (builds instant trust).
4. **Conference talks:** The personal arc IS the talk structure (Constraint -> Craft -> Contradiction -> Turn -> Commitment).
5. **Partnership pitches:** "I'm not theorising about inequality from a comfortable distance."
6. **Instagram Reel:** 60-second face-to-camera version.

## Framing Rules (from research)

- Do not position as someone who "got angry from YouTube videos." Position as someone who has lived inequality, built the skills, and is now deploying them.
- Do not overclaim: not a senior engineer, not an economist, not a finance professional.
- Frame the trading platform as: "Co-developing a trading platform gave me direct insight into how financial systems concentrate wealth."
- Frame WealthLens as: "Engineering making public data legible" — not economics commentary.
- The widening participation work is not a side detail. It IS the core of the personal narrative.
