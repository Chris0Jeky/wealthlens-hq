# Passions

## Core Drivers

- Making complex systems understandable.
- Building open-source tools that help the public inspect evidence directly.
- Connecting software engineering with opportunity, social mobility, housing, wages, and wealth.
- Helping people from underrepresented backgrounds access better information and better routes into education and work.
- Hearing what young people actually worry about — through school visits and the "From the Boys" podcast, where male students consistently raise the pressure of "providing" for their families. That anxiety is the human face of structural inequality data.

## WealthLens Angle

WealthLens should make hidden data visible without becoming partisan. The work should feel rigorous, public-spirited, and useful to ordinary people as well as researchers, journalists, educators, and campaigners.
