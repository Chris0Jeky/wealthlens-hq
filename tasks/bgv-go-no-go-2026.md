## 2. The opportunity

- **Terms:** £60,000 upfront for 7% equity, non-negotiable, plus follow-on
  funding (pre-seed approx £250-300k, seed approx £500-750k, Series A/B approx
  £500k) into the strongest 10-15 portfolio ventures/year, backed by a £33m fund
  [1][2][3][4]. (Some secondary sources cite "up to £1m" follow-on; current
  per-stage figures are from the official offer page [2][3].)
- **Format:** 6-week intensive hybrid programme (Wed mornings online, Fri
  afternoons in-person at BGV's London offices) plus approx 6 weeks coaching;
  approx 8-10 hrs/week across 2-3 days [1][2].
- **Theme fit:** Strong. BGV's "Inclusive Society" theme covers poverty
  reduction, social justice, and civic participation, and the apply page lists
  "Reducing inequality" as a selection criterion, prizing "accessibility or
  affordability that reach the underserved and underrepresented" [1][2][5]. The
  closest portfolio analogues to a wealth-inequality data tool are Organise
  (worker-rights data and campaigning) and Commonplace (civic-participation /
  transparency) [5].
- **Competitiveness:** approx 3-4% acceptance (approx 10-15 selected from
  400-500+ applicants); each application reviewed by 4+ people [1][6]. Over 70%
  of past applicants had at least a prototype/MVP, so v0.1 live is at-or-near the
  typical bar [6].
- **Timeline:** Applications close **21 June 2026, 23:59 BST**; interviews
  (online) 8-15 July 2026; offers July 2026; programme starts September 2026 [1].

## 3. The two structural blockers

**(a) For-profit Ltd only vs open-source / CIC instinct.**
Real and binding. BGV states verbatim "We only invest in for-profit companies
limited by shares" and "we cannot invest into CICs" [1]. A charity or
company-limited-by-guarantee cannot issue the equity BGV buys, so they are de
facto ineligible (this charity exclusion is an inference from the equity model,
not a verbatim "no charities" line) [1][2]. **To clear it:** WealthLens does not
need to be incorporated *to apply* ("You don't need to be incorporated... before
you apply" [1]), but to receive investment it must become a UK for-profit company
limited by shares [1]. That is a deliberate constitutional choice for an
open-source, non-partisan project, not a form-filling step. Open-source code and
a for-profit Ltd can coexist (open-core/services models exist), but it forecloses
the CIC/charity path.

**(b) Solo founder.**
Real, and a soft-to-hard gate. BGV accepts solo applicants ("Yes, you can apply
as an individual, but it's more likely that we'll invest in you with a team") but
states the dealbreaker plainly: "We definitely wouldn't invest in a founder who
thought they could do it all on their own" [1]. A solo founder with no team and
no plan to build one is a listed rejection reason [6]. **To clear it:** either
recruit a co-founder/complementary team-member, or present a concrete, credible
plan to add one. Chris's solo status is not an automatic disqualifier, but
combined with the heavy AI-assisted build pattern it weakens the "committed,
complementary team that has worked together" signal BGV scores on [1][6].

## 4. Trade-offs

- **7% equity + for-profit Ltd:** giving up 7% of a company-limited-by-shares is
  cheap capital in VC terms, but it permanently injects an equity investor with a
  contractual expectation of approx £100-200m enterprise value in 8-10 years [6]
  into a project whose current identity is "open-source always, non-partisan,
  data-first."
- **Mission-drift risk (real):** BGV explicitly favours ventures where impact and
  commercial success "go hand in hand" [6]. A wealth-inequality tool must then
  carry a credible revenue engine; "impact-only ideas without a revenue engine
  fit poorly" and are a stated rejection reason [6]. Pursuing this forces a
  commercial model now, and a non-partisan data project taking VC equity invites
  questions about independence.
- **Upside (real and large):** £60k cash for a solo unfunded founder, a
  tech-for-good investor network, B-Corp credibility, and direct progress on
  "make it visible" [3]. Follow-on capacity and 4+ expert reviewers reading the
  pitch are valuable even if rejected.
