# A pathway explorer for London WP teams

**Build a single tool that takes a postcode and returns the things students, parents, teachers and your own WP staff currently piece together by hand from a dozen PDFs and spreadsheets.** The opportunity is concrete: no public UK product combines area context (POLAR4 / TUNDRA / IMD), provider-level destinations, LEO earnings *filtered by background*, bursary eligibility, and a cross-scheme WP programme finder. Office for Students data is technically open but practically fragmented; HEAT serves staff but has no student-facing portal; Zero Gravity is gated and commercial; Discover Uni is course-level only. A standalone Python/FastAPI + Vue 3 build is achievable in a weekend (read-only postcode lookup), demonstrable as a 3-month MVP with real APP-aligned analytics, and credible as a 6–12 month funded product that plugs into TASO's incoming Higher Education Evaluation Library (HEEL) and your WealthLens UK inequality work. This brief gives you scopes, architecture, datasets with URLs, risks, and funding routes you can take to the team next week.

## Why this is the right gap to fill

Practitioners and students hit the same wall from opposite sides. A Year 12 in Newham can find their POLAR4 quintile on the OfS postcode tool, then has to leave it and visit Discover Uni for earnings, then UCAS for courses, then each university website for contextual offers, then Blackbullion (if their university subscribes) or charity sites for bursaries, then Sutton Trust / Brilliant Club / Zero Gravity / IntoUniversity / upReach individually to test programme eligibility. Your WP team simultaneously runs Eventbrite for sign-ups, HEAT for longitudinal tracking, Excel for cohort lists, Power BI for APP reporting, and bespoke postcode checkers on the public site — five tools where one consented data flow would suffice. **The OfS Equality of Opportunity Risk Register makes "information and guidance" Risk 2 — your tool is a direct response to a named regulatory risk.**

Critically, the data layer to fix this is already open under OGL v3 and CC BY: postcodes.io (postcode → LSOA), MHCLG IMD 2019 (LSOA → deprivation decile), the OfS postcode CSV (POLAR4/TUNDRA/ABCS), the DfE Explore Education Statistics API (LEO earnings by FSM × POLAR × provider × subject), GIAS (school directory with FSM%), and the Discover Uni dataset (NSS, Graduate Outcomes, continuation, tariff). The novel work is **structuring the unstructured** — university contextual-offer policies, bursary criteria, and WP-programme eligibility — into a JSON schema and exposing it through one API.

## What to build: Pathway Explorer plus a practitioner mirror

The product has two faces sharing one backend. The **student/parent-facing Pathway Explorer** takes a postcode (and optionally school URN, year group, intended subjects, FSM status, care/estranged/refugee flags) and returns: your area context (POLAR4, TUNDRA, IMD decile, IDACI, school FSM%); a curated list of universities whose contextual-admission policies you'd meet, with their entry tariffs and continuation rates; LEO median earnings for similar students by subject and provider — explicitly filtered by FSM/POLAR quintile so the comparison is honest; bursaries you'd be eligible for at each of those universities; cross-scheme WP programmes (Sutton Trust Pathways, Brilliant Club Scholars, Zero Gravity, Causeway, your own summer schools) you can apply to right now, with deadlines and direct links; and a "what next" action card. The **WP team dashboard** uses the same backend to track demographic reach against APP targets, flag under-served wards within your catchment, log event engagement via QR-code check-in with real-time POLAR/IMD enrichment, capture consent and parental-permission state, and export to HEAT (CSV), Power BI, and the future TASO HEEL submission format. **Position the tool as HEAT-complementary, not HEAT-replacing** — HEAT keeps the sector longitudinal record; your tool fixes the event-day and student-facing layer HEAT was never built for.

## The weekend prototype: a single honest postcode lookup

Scope ruthlessly. In a weekend you build **"Postcode → Pathway Snapshot"**: one Vue 3 page with a postcode input that returns a single shareable card. The card shows POLAR4 quintile, TUNDRA quintile, IMD decile (IDACI separately for under-16s), the dominant secondary school for that postcode with its FSM% from GIAS, and three contextually-matched London universities pulled from a hand-curated JSON file of 19 London providers' contextual policies. **Mock everything that can't be done in 48 hours**: bursary data is a small static JSON, "eligible programmes" is a hardcoded list of five named schemes with simple rules, LEO is a single subject (Computing or Law, where the spread is most dramatic — £32k between top and bottom decile provider per LEO 2022-23). Backend is FastAPI with three endpoints (`/lookup/{postcode}`, `/providers`, `/programmes`); frontend is one Vue 3 SFC with a results panel and a "Show your WP team" share button. Deploy free on Fly.io or Railway. The demo line is: *"This took one weekend and uses only open data. Now imagine it with your APP cohort logic and HEAT integration."* That single artefact is what gets the team meeting on the calendar.

## The three-month MVP: real data, real cohorts, deployable

The MVP turns the demo into a production-grade tool you can put in front of a real outreach cohort. The student-facing side gains the full postcode → contextual offers matrix for **all London 19 universities plus the Russell Group** (a one-engineer scrape-and-structure exercise — contextual policies are publicly published, just in PDFs and web pages), live LEO data via the EES CSV-by-GUID API filtered by FSM and POLAR quintile, a bursary database covering the same providers (start with England's published Access and Participation Plan financial-support sections, which are structured spend lines), and a WP-programme eligibility engine covering the **eight or nine flagship national schemes** (Sutton Trust Pathways and Summer Schools, Brilliant Club Scholars, Zero Gravity, Causeway, IntoUniversity, upReach, your own summer school, Aimhigher London). Add a "save my pathway" feature with email-based magic links — no accounts, no minor-data risk.

The practitioner side becomes usable: a Vue 3 admin dashboard with QR-code event check-in (mobile-first), instant postcode enrichment on registration, consent and parental-permission capture aligned to your DPIA, cohort demographic breakdown against your APP targets and the OfS Equality of Opportunity Risk Register's 12 risks, an APP-target progress view, and CSV/Excel export in HEAT's bulk-upload format. Add Theory of Change capture using TASO's templates and MOAT activity classification so every logged event is automatically tagged for Type 1 narrative reporting and ready for Type 2 empirical analysis later. **Deliverables at month three**: a public demo URL, a live internal staging instance with one consented cohort, a 10-page write-up against APP and EORR risks, a DPIA, and a published GitHub repo (MIT or AGPL).

## The 6-12 month funded build: a sector-credible product

With internal funding (HEIF, APP investment line, internal innovation/seedcorn — the fastest route) plus a small external grant, the 6-12 month vision extends in three directions. **Scope nationally**: all 140+ English HE providers' contextual policies and bursaries, structured as a queryable schema with a published JSON Schema others can contribute to; coverage of all four UK nations' deprivation indices (IMD 2019/2025, WIMD 2019, SIMD 2020, NIMDM 2017). **Evidence-grade evaluation**: built-in randomisation and treatment/control assignment infrastructure so the tool can host TASO-aligned Type 3 evaluations of digital outreach interventions — a documented sector gap TASO's Institutional Data Use Project explicitly flagged. **Sector integration**: a published HEAT export adapter, a TASO HEEL submission feed (HEEL launches in 2026 and will need partners), an embeddable widget for school websites and Unifrog, and a Discover Uni widget integration. Position the funded build as **infrastructure that reduces evaluation cost for every provider** — that framing aligns with how OfS is reshaping funding post-2025-26 SPG cut.

## Technical architecture you can defend in one slide

The stack is appropriate, defensible, and matches your skills. **Backend**: FastAPI with Pydantic models for every public schema (Postcode, AreaContext, ProviderPolicy, Bursary, Programme), async httpx for upstream calls, Redis (or SQLite for v1) cache for postcode lookups (TTL by source — postcodes.io: 30 days, OfS postcode CSV: pinned by release date), Postgres with PostGIS for any spatial queries, Alembic for migrations, pytest with property-based tests for the eligibility rules engine. **Frontend**: Vue 3 with `<script setup>`, Pinia for state, Vite, Tailwind for speed, Chart.js or D3 for the wealth/earnings pipeline visualisation that links to WealthLens UK, and `@vueuse/head` for SEO. **Data ingestion**: a small ETL package (Prefect or even a cron'd Python script) that pulls EES CSV-by-GUID for LEO, refreshes the OfS postcode CSV on each annual release, refreshes IMD when MHCLG updates it, and re-scrapes contextual policies quarterly with human-in-the-loop validation. **Hosting**: Fly.io or Hetzner for cost; Cloudflare in front; everything containerised; CI in GitHub Actions; observability via Sentry. **Auth**: minimal — public side anonymous with optional magic-link save; staff side SSO via your university's Azure AD/Entra. **Eligibility engine**: implement as declarative JSON-Logic or a small DSL so non-engineers in the WP team can maintain rules (this is the single highest-leverage architectural decision — keep eligibility logic *out of code*).

## The data layer with URLs you can paste into a sprint plan

Postcode geocoding uses **postcodes.io** (`https://api.postcodes.io/postcodes/{postcode}`, MIT, no key). Area classifications come from the OfS postcode CSV at `officeforstudents.org.uk/data-and-analysis/young-participation-by-area/get-the-postcode-data/` (refreshed Feb 2026 — POLAR4, TUNDRA MSOA, TUNDRA LSOA, ABCS quintiles). IMD 2019 deciles ship from `imd-by-postcode.opendatacommunities.org` or the raw LSOA file at `gov.uk/government/statistics/english-indices-of-deprivation-2019`; check whether the IMD 2025 update is now the authoritative version. LEO comes from Explore Education Statistics using their permanent CSV-by-GUID URLs — `explore-education-statistics.service.gov.uk/find-statistics/leo-graduate-and-postgraduate-outcomes/2022-23` and the provider-level companion. School data lives in GIAS (`get-information-schools.service.gov.uk`, public API) and Compare School Performance. Course-level data comes from the Discover Uni dataset ZIP at `hesa.ac.uk/support/tools-and-downloads/unistats` (CC BY 4.0, weekly updates). The OfS Access and Participation data dashboard bulk CSVs at `officeforstudents.org.uk/data-and-analysis/access-and-participation-data-dashboard/about-the-data-dashboard/get-the-data/` underpin the staff-side APP target view; the Equality of Opportunity Risk Register taxonomy comes from the same site. **Watch-outs**: postcode quintile reuse is frozen by OfS to preserve consistency; NI postcodes are LPS-licensed and not redistributable, so the v1 explicitly scopes England-only with Wales/Scotland/NI as roadmap items; HEAT pricing and integration require a paid membership conversation with the University of Kent team that operates it.

## Risks, ethics and GDPR — the section that gets it past Information Governance

Three risks dominate and each has a clean mitigation.

**Minors and consent**. Anyone under 13 is high-risk under GDPR Article 8 and the ICO Children's Code. Mitigation: the public Pathway Explorer takes no personal identifiers, just postcode and self-reported context, and stores nothing without an explicit magic-link save (over-13 only, with an age-gate page and parental-permission flow for the WP team's tracked cohorts). All staff-side tracking flows through a DPIA you complete with your DPO before the MVP goes live; lawful basis is Public Task (Article 6(1)(e)) tied to the OfS APP statutory duty, with consent layered on top for parental permission of under-16s in school programmes. Apply data minimisation aggressively: never store full postcode for tracked individuals — store LSOA only — and never store DOB, only year-of-birth or year-group.

**Deficit framing**. Telling a Year 11 they live in a POLAR Q1 area can either expand their horizons or pathologise them. The dominant academic critique of POLAR4 (Boliver, Sutton Trust 2021, UCL WP Group 2022) is the ecological fallacy — affluent households inside Q1 MSOAs get flagged, and vice versa. Mitigation: never present quintiles as labels on the student; present them as information about *their area's history of HE participation* and *what universities have committed to do about it*. Use the framing "Here are 12 universities that have published a contextual offer for someone in your area" rather than "You are a WP student." Co-design copy with student panels — Brilliant Club's Parent Power model is the template.

**Algorithmic eligibility**. An eligibility engine that wrongly tells a student they're not eligible for the Sutton Trust Pathways is potentially more harmful than no tool. Mitigation: never close doors. The engine returns "likely eligible / possibly eligible / cannot confirm — apply directly" with explicit links and an explanation of which criterion was the limiting factor. Log every "not eligible" outcome for human review. Publish the rules as open JSON. Add a "tell us we got this wrong" feedback loop reviewed weekly during MVP.

Additional considerations: full WCAG 2.2 AA accessibility from day one (your audience disproportionately includes disabled learners; this is also a legal requirement for public-sector bodies); a published privacy notice in plain English tested with Year 10s; a documented data retention schedule; supplier due diligence on any third-party (Sentry, Cloudflare, postcodes.io — though postcodes.io self-hostable removes that risk); and an algorithmic transparency record published alongside the tool.

## Funding routes ranked by feasibility

Realistically the 6-12 month build is funded **internally** through a combination of HEIF, your APP investment line, and your university's internal innovation/seedcorn fund — typically £5–50k and decision-able in weeks. Frame the ask as: *this tool services Risks 1, 2, 4, 7 and 12 on the OfS EORR; it reduces our APP evaluation cost; it produces Type 2-ready data; here is the DPIA and a 12-week MVP already deployed.* That is a much stronger internal pitch than a cold external grant.

External grants follow internal commitment. The most credible external routes are:

- **A delivery-charity partnership bid into Comic Relief / Paul Hamlyn Tech for Good when it next reopens** — historically £5k Explore / £70k Build over 9 months, delivered with CAST acceleration support. Pair with Causeway Education or Brilliant Club as the charity applicant; your university is the technical partner.
- **Paul Hamlyn Ideas and Pioneers Fund** — up to £20k + 18 months, but only via a partner organisation since universities are typically ineligible.
- **TASO commissioned-evaluator route** — not a build fund, but pitch the tool as data infrastructure for a TASO-commissioned Type 3 RCT of digital outreach. Watch `taso.org.uk/news-blog` for open calls; the HEEL 2026 launch will likely tender partners.
- **Aimhigher London sub-commission via Uni Connect** — capped at £20m nationally for 2025-26 but partnerships do sub-commission services; an obvious fit for London.
- **OfS Challenge Competitions** — currently under review post-SPG cut; the next call is plausible in late 2026 once OfS's funding-approach consultation concludes. Watch `officeforstudents.org.uk/advice-and-guidance/funding-for-providers/funding-competitions/`.

Avoid Esmée Fairbairn (university R&D excluded), Garfield Weston (charity-only), Innovate UK (commercial), and ESRC unless reframed as academic-led research. The Education Innovation Awards 2026 (£50k, no AI) is plausible if the build is owned by a non-profit reaching ≥2,000 learners.

## The WealthLens UK connection makes this strategic

The same datasets power both projects. IMD, LEO, ONS Wealth and Assets Survey, ASHE, FRS and Understanding Society sit underneath WealthLens UK's wealth-inequality story and Pathway Explorer's WP story. **The visualisation argument is the connective tissue**: educational inequality is not a separate problem from wealth inequality — it is the *first valve* in a multi-stage funnel. Each stage is quantifiable: a Year 11 FSM pupil is roughly 60% as likely to enter HE as a non-FSM peer; the FSM graduate-earnings gap five years out is 8–12% per LEO 2022-23; the class pay gap inside elite occupations is 16% (≈£7,350/year per Friedman & Laurison); the IFS 2026 wealth recalculation shows degree-holders' median wealth at £420k vs £66k for those with no qualifications in 2020–22; and Resolution Foundation shows inheritances doubling over 20 years and flowing disproportionately to the top quintile. **Build the Pathway Explorer with a sister visualisation — a Sankey or cumulative-wealth-by-stage chart — that compounds these eight statistics into a single picture of how WP access today maps to wealth distribution in 40 years.** That is the artefact that turns a postcode tool into a piece of public-interest infrastructure, and it is the framing that wins philanthropic funding. Universities UK's own London Economics work claims £14 of economic benefit per £1 of public HE investment; IFS estimates the average exchequer return per graduate at £100k+. **WP is not charity, it is the highest-NPV social investment the state makes** — that is the line that gets you in front of senior stakeholders.

## Conclusion: the build is small, the framing is big

Almost everything you need to ship a credible v1 exists and is open: postcodes.io for geography, OfS for area classifications, EES for LEO, GIAS for schools, Discover Uni for courses, MHCLG for IMD. The genuinely novel work is **a structured eligibility schema** for contextual offers, bursaries and WP programmes — and that is a single-engineer, three-month exercise. The strategic insight is that this is not a new CRM (HEAT exists), not a new mentoring platform (Zero Gravity exists), and not a new course tool (Discover Uni exists). It is the *missing connective layer* between them, and the OfS Equality of Opportunity Risk Register has effectively pre-written its business case. Build the weekend prototype this Saturday, demo it to the WP team on Tuesday, file the DPIA in week two, and you have a defensible path to internal funding by month three, an MVP serving real cohorts by month six, and a sector-credible Higher Education Evaluation Library partner by month twelve — all while feeding the same data spine that powers your WealthLens UK story about how unequal valves at age 18 compound into unequal wealth at age 60.
