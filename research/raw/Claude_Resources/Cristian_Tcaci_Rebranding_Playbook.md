# Cristian Tcaci — Rebranding Playbook

## The Core Narrative

Everything flows from one story: **you're a software engineer who builds systems that make inequality visible and fixable.** You didn't come from privilege. You worked your way to a First Class degree, a Springer publication, and production experience building trading infrastructure. Now you're turning those exact skills — the ones that usually serve the wealthy — toward the public interest. You work with underprivileged kids through widening participation because you know the system from both sides.

This isn't a career pivot. It's a mission that your career serves.

### About Section

I'm a software engineer who builds systems that make inequality visible.

I grew up understanding what it means when your family can't afford things other families take for granted. I studied computer science, earned a First Class degree, published peer-reviewed research on cooperation and game theory (Springer, SGAI-AI 2025), and spent 15 months at GE Digital integrating security into enterprise infrastructure. I've built trading platforms and developer tools with nearly 4,000 commits of production code.

Now I'm turning those skills toward something that matters more.

I'm building open-source data tools that make UK wealth inequality data — the kind buried in ONS spreadsheets and HMRC reports — accessible, interactive, and impossible to ignore. The data exists. The problem is that almost nobody can see it.

Alongside this, I work with Middlesex University's Widening Participation team, running workshops and outreach for young people from underrepresented backgrounds who deserve a shot at higher education. I've been that kid. I know what a single conversation can change.

I believe technology should serve the public, not just the privileged. If you're working on economic justice, tax reform, or public data infrastructure — or if you're an organisation that could use engineering support — I'd love to connect.

### Content Strategy (3 posts/week)

**Monday: Technical build update**
Share what you built that week on WealthLens. Show a chart, explain a data finding, link to the repo. "This week I visualised 20 years of UK wealth concentration data from ONS. The top 10% now hold X% of all wealth. Here's the interactive version: [link]"

**Wednesday: Inequality insight or data point**
Take a statistic from your research and make it human. "The gap in total wealth between the top 10% and bottom 10% in the UK increased by 48% between 2011 and 2019. I'm building tools to make data like this impossible to ignore."

**Friday: Widening participation or personal story**
Share a moment from your outreach work (anonymised). "Yesterday I spoke to a 16-year-old who assumed university wasn't for people like him. I was that kid. Here's what I told him." These posts will get the most engagement by far.

**Rules:**
- No external links in the main post (kills reach). Put links in the first comment.
- Write in your voice, not corporate-speak.
- End with a question or call to action ("What data would you want to see?")
- Engage with every comment within 30 minutes of posting.
- Use hashtags sparingly: #WealthInequality #OpenData #EconomicJustice #WideningParticipation

## The Widening Participation Connection

This is the emotional core of your brand, and you should lean into it hard. Here's why it's so powerful:

You're not a privileged tech worker who discovered inequality from a YouTube video. You're someone who experienced the consequences of inequality directly, worked to build yourself up through education and engineering, and is now using those skills to fight the system from within — while simultaneously helping the next generation of underprivileged kids access the same opportunities.

That story arc is:
**Experienced inequality → Built technical skills → Used those skills in finance/enterprise → Turned them toward justice → Now helps others do the same**

When you write your LSE personal statement (if you apply), when you email Gary, when you pitch to Tax Justice UK — this is the narrative spine. Not "I watched some YouTube videos and got angry." It's "I've lived this, I built the skills, and now I'm deploying them."

Specific ways to connect WP to your broader mission:
- Write a LinkedIn post: "I work in widening participation because I was the kid who didn't think university was for him. Now I build tools that show *why* those kids feel that way — and the data is damning."
- Create a simple data visualisation showing university access by household income decile (UCAS publishes this data) and share it as part of your WP work.
- Propose to your university's WP team that you build an interactive tool for their outreach: "Where do students from your postcode end up?" using HESA destination data. This serves your WP job *and* your broader mission *and* your portfolio.
